This page helps you develop your decision's Critical Concepts, which are the one, two or three things that really matter to you. They answer: 'What am I really solving for?' There's no one right answer. Two people looking at the same data may well have different Critical Concepts and may make entirely different decisions.

Think of your decision like a poker hand. How do you increase your odds of winning â€“ of choosing your best path forward? You've got incomplete information, and the future will change in some ways you can predict and many you can't. But as the pros at a poker table will tell you (unless they're playing against you), you don't have to know everything because you only have to focus on what hands your cards can beat.

What are the outcomes that matter for you in this game? Every poker player has a set of skills but they start each hand fresh, figuring out what's critical for that hand. The same will be true for you as a decision maker developing your decision's Critical Concepts. As they say in poker, that's where you're "pot-committed." You've got chips on the table and a vested interest. The game is highly personal, subjective and specific to your situation.
