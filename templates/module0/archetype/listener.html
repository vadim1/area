Strengths

In life, there are few things more powerful than having a tight network of people who support you. As a Listener, your community is one of your biggest assets. You have people who you trust to help you make decisions and move forward in your life, and you know that you can always turn to them. That is a great position to be in. In general, you are a trusting person who believes in the people around you. You have no trouble building relationships with people and continuing to expand your network of support.

Blind Spots

Being a Listener means that you may rely too heavily on other people to make decisions for you. Because you are loyal to those you trust, you can have a blind spot and make decisions based on what—or who!— you like, rather than on what's actually best. This is called the "liking bias." In addition because you like to listen to others, hearing their stories may lead you to the “narrative bias,” where you overvalue storylines and undervalue data. When you pay too much attention to what others are thinking and doing, you stop thinking for yourself. This "social proof bias" means that you just do what others recommend, instead of thinking about what you actually want.
