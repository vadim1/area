Strengths

Being the Detective means that you are committed to learning, which means that you tend to have more information and be better informed than the people around you. You are focused when you are faced with a decision. You don't let your emotions distract you, and that means you can thoughtfully look at the evidence around you. You are down to earth, and you don't settle for nonsense. You don't have a problem making a decision once you feel like you have the right information.

Blind Spots

Being a Detective comes with a few drawbacks, too. Sometimes, because you are gathering so much evidence, the information can blend together and you can lose your ability to be objective. More information does not always make for a better decision. If you use new information to confirm what you already thought you may fall prey to "confirmation bias." Although you are driven by the facts, sometimes when you make decisions about the future, you assume that your preferences are going to stay the same forever. This "projection bias" makes you interpret the facts based on an outdated idea of success. Finally, because you like to soak up lots of information, you sometimes don't do a good job with time management; this "planning fallacy" means that you get so focused on learning that you don't make your decision on time.
